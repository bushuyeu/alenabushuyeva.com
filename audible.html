Scope of the project


Competitive and visual analysis, user research, user flows, low and high fidelity wireframing and prototypes of web and mobile applications

Audible is an Amazon company that produces and sells hundreds of thousands of popular audiobooks and Audible Original titles. It is the leading creator and provider of premium audio storytelling, enriching the lives of millions of listeners every day. With a customer-centric approach to technological innovation and superior programming, Audible has reinvented a media category and is the driving force behind today’s audio entertainment revolution.

Currently, Amazon Audible hosts 250,000 audiobooks narrated by 2,000 trained narrators, including celebrities from around the world.

Why I decided to redesign Audible



I believe that the benefits of listening to audiobooks are extensive. Audiobooks are immersive, educational, instructional, entertaining. They’re perfect for the time-rich and the time-poor. Studies are showing that there are numerous benefits to regularly listening to audiobooks, including:

• increasing reading accuracy by 52%
• increasing reading speed, expanding vocabulary, and improving fluency
• teaching pronunciation
• improving comprehension by 76%; and
• raising test scores by 21% when engaged in multi-modal learning.
Not to mention:
• 27% of the kindergarten to year 12 population are auditory learners
• students can listen and comprehend two grade levels above their reading level when regularly listening to audiobooks; and
• combining print and audio increases recall by 40% over print alone.
Listening is the first language skill that we acquire, and 85% of what we learn, we learn by listening. For those young learners who have difficulty reading, listening to audiobooks can make learning a much easier process.

Quantitative Research



I’ve started with a hypothesis that users struggled with a lack of filtration options. Reddit’s appeared to be the focal point of the online community, so it was my main source for user-generated information and a good place to start validating my assumptions.



I also have posted an Instagram story that I am currently transferring into UX Research/ UX Interaction specifically and researching the audiobooks market. I needed to analyze data to better understand listener attitudes, organizational habits, needs, and pain points of users. I’ve also asked to tag every person my followers know who is the biggest fan of audiobooks and who would like to share their user experience with me. To aggregate data, I’ve asked to complete a survey and have received 277 responses.

Qualitative Research



For the second phase, I’ve invited 20 Audible users on a virtual coffee and have interviewed them about their Audible experience via Zoom. I have also asked my sponsor users to show me how they do several user scenarios: browsing for the next book to listen to, filtering, and evaluating audiobooks in search results and category pages.

Empathy map



To better empathize and synthesize my observations from the research phase and draw out audible users’ insights on an empathy map.
