Scope of the project


UI Design

I created the visual and interaction design of the website based on the user research and competitive analysis taking inspiration from other products and services that share similar values.


UX Design

Following the research phase, I created the information architecture, low fidelity prototype, conducted a competitive and visual study to address the findings, and handed the final result to the development team.


Flow of the project

Challenges


1. While the housing industry is booming in the US, exclusive real estate projects are still challenging to sell.

2. The architectural studio is interested to keep a detailed overview of their projects in their portfolio to showcase their best work and attract future customers.

3. The website should hold an exclusive spirit of the real estate and of the architectural studio.

4. Both the house hunting and house selling process can be very time-consuming and involve an overload of information. The website should provide just enough information about the real estate and rather form an emotional attachment that would speak for this property.

Discovery


User research


The user research was carried out by conducting interviews and sending out surveys to a range of existing and prospective customers of the architectural studio to gain insight into how they perceived an ideal home-buying experience and what information is vital for the decision making to purchase a property.


Empathy map


Stakeholder, product and industry research


The stakeholder research was carried out by conducting interviews with the owners of the architectural studio and professionals in the industry: builders, realtors, architects. This helped me understand the company's perspective on the most important features of the project and get a professional opinion on the market perspective.

The initial task from the client was to design a multi-page website for the product. After I conducted a visual, comparative, and competitive analysis, it was clear that a simpler, one page landing page will be an effective and less time-consuming project. Landing page is a perfect tool for describing a specific product. It guides visitors towards a single call to action. As our main target was to design a page for conversion, we don’t need to create a rocket, when the client is asking for a bike :)

Lessons learned

Good UX is key


Research is a fundamental part that contains about 80% of the whole process. If you want to get smooth cooperation with the client with fewer iterations, you should prepare a good base, know the product and know potential customers before stepping into the UI design part.


Don’t over-promise

One more important lesson is that you always want to be above and beyond for your team, but never set up an unrealistic timeframe. Be transparent and be ready to explain the whole process and why you can’t eliminate particular research parts. Even better — involve people from the team in the process.


Minimum costs, maximum results

Avoid costly and unrealistic design decisions.
